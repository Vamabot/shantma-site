9:T613,<p>Leverage is the ratio of output to input. High leverage means a small input produces a large output.</p>
<h2>Types of leverage</h2>
<p><strong>Labor</strong> — The oldest form. Hire people to multiply your output. Limited by coordination costs and management bandwidth.</p>
<p><strong>Capital</strong> — Money making money. Deploy capital into assets or ventures. Requires trust (or collateral).</p>
<p><strong>Code</strong> — Software runs without sleep. One codebase can serve millions. Zero marginal cost at scale.</p>
<p><strong>Media</strong> — Ideas, content, reputation. Something you make once that continues working. A book, a video, a framework that spreads.</p>
<p>The newest forms of leverage — code and media — are available to anyone with internet access. This is historically unusual.</p>
<h2>The key insight</h2>
<p>Most people optimize for income (hourly rate × hours). High-earners optimize for leverage (output per unit of effort).</p>
<p>The question to ask: "What would let me produce 10x more output from the same hour?"</p>
<h2>Leverage and responsibility</h2>
<p>High leverage amplifies both good and bad decisions. A mistake at low leverage costs you an afternoon. The same mistake at high leverage can cost a company.</p>
<p>This is why judgment matters more as leverage increases. You need to be right more often, because being wrong is more expensive.</p>
