Leverage is the ratio of output to input. High leverage means a small input produces a large output.

## Types of leverage

**Labor** — The oldest form. Hire people to multiply your output. Limited by coordination costs and management bandwidth.

**Capital** — Money making money. Deploy capital into assets or ventures. Requires trust (or collateral).

**Code** — Software runs without sleep. One codebase can serve millions. Zero marginal cost at scale.

**Media** — Ideas, content, reputation. Something you make once that continues working. A book, a video, a framework that spreads.

The newest forms of leverage — code and media — are available to anyone with internet access. This is historically unusual.

## The key insight

Most people optimize for income (hourly rate × hours). High-earners optimize for leverage (output per unit of effort).

The question to ask: "What would let me produce 10x more output from the same hour?"

## Leverage and responsibility

High leverage amplifies both good and bad decisions. A mistake at low leverage costs you an afternoon. The same mistake at high leverage can cost a company.

This is why judgment matters more as leverage increases. You need to be right more often, because being wrong is more expensive.
